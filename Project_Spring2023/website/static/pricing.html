FAQ

What types of documents can a notary public notarize?

A: Notaries can notarize a wide range of documents, including affidavits, deeds, contracts, powers of attorney, wills, vehicle title transfers, and more. However, they cannot notarize birth, death, or marriage certificates, as these are typically issued by government agencies.

Do I need to bring identification when meeting with a notary?

A: Yes, you must bring a valid, government-issued photo ID, such as a driver's license, passport, or state-issued identification card. This helps the notary verify your identity and ensures a secure and compliant notarization process.

How do you ensure the security of our digital assets and data?

A: We take the security of your digital assets and data seriously, employing industry-leading encryption protocols, multi-factor authentication, and strict access controls to protect your sensitive information. We conduct regular security audits, adhere to data privacy regulations like GDPR and CCPA, and monitor our systems 24/7 for potential threats. With robust backup and recovery plans in place, we strive to provide a secure and reliable E-Notary platform, ensuring your notarization transactions are handled with the utmost care and confidence.

Can I have a document notarized remotely through your E-Notary service?

A: Yes, our E-Notary service allows you to have your documents notarized remotely through a secure online platform, providing convenience and accessibility without the need for an in-person meeting.

How long does it take to schedule a mobile notary appointment?

A: We strive to accommodate your needs as promptly as possible. Typically, a mobile notary appointment can be scheduled within 24-48 hours, depending on availability and location. In some cases, same-day appointments may also be possible.

What should I do if I don't have a valid government-issued ID for notarization?

A: If you don't have a valid government-issued ID, we recommend obtaining one before scheduling a notary appointment, as proper identification is crucial for the notarization process. In some cases, alternative forms of identification may be accepted, but it's best to contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Are your notary services available for businesses as well as individuals?

A: Absolutely! Our notary services cater to both individuals and businesses, providing convenient and secure notarization solutions for a wide range of documents and transactions, including contracts, affidavits, and more.
